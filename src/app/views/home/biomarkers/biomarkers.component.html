Research into cancer biomarkers or signals can help both researchers and clinicians.

Early cancer screening – Can we develop non-invasive tests that can find cancer while it can be effectively treated? Can we find multiple cancer types by looking for circulating tumor DNA or other tumor associated molecules or cells?

Monitoring treatment response – Today, we rely on imaging-based tests such as with x-rays for monitoring tumor
status. Successful biomarkers will allow researchers to see the treatment response without waiting for the tumor size
change to be visible.

Predicting treatment response – Researchers can also use biomarkers to more precisely identify patient
subpopulations who will respond to a treatment. Or to better predict who might experience serious side-effects.

Guiding immunotherapy research – Measurement of molecular and cellular immunologic signatures during trials,
and sharing of collected data and insights will accelerate development of new immunotherapy for canines.
