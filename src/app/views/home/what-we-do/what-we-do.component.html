WE SUPPORT CANINE CANCER RESEARCH

We launch projects that bring together teams of scientists, engineers, veterinarians,
and pet owners to address the following challenges.

PREVENTION

What can we do to keep our pets healthy? What factors that are under our control (nutrition, supplements, exposure to chemicals, etc) have preventive impact? Where is the scientific data?

EARLY SCREENING

How do we find cancer early, so it can be most effectively treated? We need non-invasive cancer screening tests with urine or blood samples -so called "liquid biopsy'". Reliability as well as affordability will be important factors.

TREATMENTS FOR FULL CURE

Cancer cells are supremely adaptive. They mutate and spread while developing resistance to all kinds of drugs. We want more than a few months life extension. We are seeking treatments that put dogs into full remission even after cancer has metastasized.


OUR REASONS FOR OPTIMISM

Dogs are not people. But naturally occurring tumors in dogs have many biological similarities to human cancers. Similarities also extend to the way our immune systems interact with cancer. And in the past decade, enormous advances have been made in new therapies and technologies to help tackle human cancer. These innovations can be brought to bear to help dogs.

We are reaching out to researchers and biotech companies that work on human cancers to see if they could also help dogs. And we are getting many positive responses. Why? Because many researchers love dogs. And because if they can show that a new therapy is helping dogs, it can pave the way to accelerate introduction to people. It's a win-win for canine and human patients.
